Intro

Throughout my studies at DePaul and my experience in my field, I have learned to love front-end development and creating solutions for clients. While I can't show all of my work here today due to NDA's and other inhibitors, I hope that I am able to showcase to you my passion for web design.

Work

DePaul ID Lab Website

This project was created using html, css, php, bootstrap, javascript, and jQuery. This is a tech stack that I was unfamiliar with prior to the creation of this site, but since then I have learned how to make cross-platform designs that work on all devices. I have been able to transfer the knowledge that I gained from this project to my career and it even aided in building the you're currently on.

During this project, there was a lot of turnover within my team and we had several cooks in the kitchen for the design of the site. This proved challenging as I had to quickly pivot between designs that were being made in our weekly sprint meetings. I think that when all was said and done, it turned out well but I definitely learned why it is important to have a core design team when creating this type of site. Being able to keep a clear and consistent vision for development helps keep the team aligned throughout the process and helps everyone work towards one vision.

ODI 2022 Website

This site was simplified down due to my learning from previous projects. The site utilizes a clean one page design that only uses html, css, bootstrap, and minimal javascript. The goal of the site was to create a page for a conference that displayed the relevant info and how to sign up in a concise way that was easy to navigate for all types of users on all devices. By building this project, I was able to further my knowledge of front-end development which is important for my goals of becoming a full-stack developer/software engineer.

This project tested my knowledge of responsive design. Despite this website being a singular page, adjusting the images, multimedia, and sponsorship branding proved to be quite difficult to what I expected. Outside of this, having to make sure that the speakers section did not have any bugs was also a tedious task due to the number of speakers and information. Besides the editing of the site itself, the nature of the project helped me realize why it is important to have good work flows in my teams. Since information was constantly changing and being adjusted as we found speakers/sponsors, it became increasingly important to have a build process for deploying our site that had version control to ensure we always had a live site without bugs.
